Fireworks

A fun rule that looks like fireworks when started from a single seed. This rule is similar to Life, but a cell births whenever the sum of its neighbors is even (up to a maximum of 200), and a cell dies whenever the sum of its neighbors is odd (up to a maximum of 201).

Cells can have value 0 to 50. Only cells that have state 1 are counted as "alive". When a cell dies, it slowly fades away by cycling through the higher valued states. Once the highest state is reached, the cell is set to state 0. These higher valued states take space on the grid, but they do not count as alive neighbors.

Invented by David Bahr.
