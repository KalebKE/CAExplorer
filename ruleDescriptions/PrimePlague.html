Prime Plague

Similar to Life, but creates a creeping plague. Cells are born if they have exactly 3 "alive" neighbors. They survive if they have a prime number of neighbors. Otherwise they die.

Only cells that have state 1 are counted as "alive". When a cell dies, it slowly fades away by cycling through the higher valued states. Once the highest state is reached, the cell is set to state 0. These higher valued states take space on the grid, but they do not count as alive neighbors.

Note that this is the same rule as Piston Prime which only has 4 states on an eight-neighbor square lattice. This rule has 13 states on a twelve-neighbor triangular lattice. The difference in behavior is dramatic.

Invented by David Bahr.
