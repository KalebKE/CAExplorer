Rule 102.

Calculates the sum modulo N of a cell and its neighbor to the right. The "102" comes from a cellular automata numbering scheme popularized by Stephen Wolfram (see Wolfram 1-d Rules).

On a one-dimensional (2 neighbor) lattice with a single initial state of value 1, this is a modulo N version of Pascal's triangle. Large grids (e.g., 500 X 500) are slow but can produce beautiful fractal patterns.
