Copy Random Neighbor

Copies the value of a random neighbor. Note that this does not "conserve mass" or "conserve values". Regions with a particular cell value may grow or shrink in number depending on what gets randomly copied. This growth and decay of values is particularly obvious in one-dimensional simulations.

Also note that in one-dimensional simulations running at high speed, you can occasionally see "dithering". That's when two closely interspersed colors appear to the eye as a third blended color. For example, red and blue colors mixed together in a checkerboard pattern may appear as purple.
