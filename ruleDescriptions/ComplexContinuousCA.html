Continuous CA

Amazingly "discrete" behavior emerging from cells with complex numbers (with "continuous" fractional values).  This CA appears capable of mimicking many integer-valued CA, including many of the one-dimensional Wolfram Rules with their characteristic upside down triangles. This rule is also very similar to the real-valued Continuous CA.

Each cell is averaged with all of its neighbors.  The average x is converted to y = m x + b for some slope m and intercept b.  Then, only the fractional part of y is kept. In other words, think of the complex number y in polar coordinates. The cell is assigned the same angle (complex arg), but only the fractional part of the magnitude (complex modulus) is kept. The result is a cell value that always lies within the unit circle.

Be sure to open the More Properties box and adjust the slope and intercept.  Try many values to get a sense of the possible behaviors. Notice that the behaviors encompass all of the Wolfram classes I, II, III, and IV.
